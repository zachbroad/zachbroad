## Why should I care about my site's speed?

Your website's performance is more important than ever in 2019. 

What exactly is the purpose of a website? You could argue the purpose of nearly every website on the internet is to **provide some form of information** to the user. 

The quicker you can do this, the better.

Did you know that [more than 52% of all web traffic today comes from a mobile device](https://www.statista.com/statistics/241462/global-mobile-phone-website-traffic-share/) or tablet? It's not optional — you must cater to the mobile device audience.

### Any day now...

Have you ever been in an area with poor cellular connection, but you absolutely *need* to access a certain website? 

Your Google search results finally load. You immediately click the first website before your service cuts out again just to be greeted by over a minute loading time. You refresh constantly as you try to find service.

Finally, the website loads, and to your delight, the information isn't what you're looking for. You click the back button, and repeat until you find what you're looking for.

We've *all* been there. 

#### It's time to get rid of the bloat

It's important to keep loading times low to allow your website to load in areas with sub-par network connection.

You might be wondering, what takes so long just for a few texts and images to load?!

The reality is, most modern websites aren't just a few paragraphs of text with some images. Not only will you have to load the HTML document itself, but also CSS stylesheets, JavaScript files, excessively-large images, and more.

There's a lot of bloat with most websites that is unused, yet sent with every browser request.

## People are impatient
It should come as no surprise that people are becoming more and more impatient when it comes to the web. 

Users want information and they want it *now*. 

> Did you know that **more than 50% of users will leave your site if it doesn't load in less than 3 seconds**?

Users enter their query into Google, click the top result, and wait a few seconds. If this first page doesn't load in a few seconds, they'll go onto the next result. 

Simply put, if your website's performance isn't top-notch, you're going to be losing out on a substantial amount of possible customers.

## Search Engine Rankings

Did you know that your website's speed can affect your search engine rankings? Yes, that's right, your website's performance alone can affect your placement in Google.

Search engines such as Google, Yahoo, and Bing even reward sites with fast loading times by ranking them higher in search results. 

With most average loading time for websites being around 5 seconds, having a blisteringly-quick website is sure to make you stand out from the competition.

## What makes a fast website?

There're many elements that make a performant website. 

+ Caching
+ Server-side rendering
+ Minimizing JavaScript and CSS files
+ Compression
+ Thoughtful design
