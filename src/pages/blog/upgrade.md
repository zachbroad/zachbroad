## Should I update my website?

Are you trying to decide whether it's time to upgrade your existing website?

Technology is constantly changing every day, and the web's standards are no exception. 

As the times change, people expect more and more out of the websites they visit. What was considered modern and cutting-edge in 2015 is viewed as outdated now.

In this article, I'm going to cover some of the signs that it's time to make some changes to your site.

## 1. Not mobile friendly

One of the biggest indicators that it's time to upgrade your website is if your current site isn't optimized for mobile devices such as phones and tablets.

With over 51% (sometimes closer to 70%-80% for some sites) of web traffic coming from mobile devices, having a mobile-friendly design is arguably the most important feature your website should have.

## 2. Sub-par search engine rankings

Is your website ranking as high as you'd like on search engines such as Google and Bing? 

It's important that a website is optimized in a way that search engines can easily "crawl" the pages to find what people are looking for.

As updates are made to these search engines' algorithms, your website may not perform as well as it used to. 

## 3. Slow performance
The quicker your website, the better.

Did you know that **more than 50% of users will leave your site if it doesn't load in less than 3 seconds**?

If you're lucky enough to have somebody click on your website, your site only have a few seconds to load before they leave and find another site.

Search engines such as Google, Yahoo, and Bing even reward sites with fast loading times by ranking them higher in search results. 

With most average loading time for websites being around 5 seconds, having a blisteringly-quick website is sure to make you stand out from the competition.


## 4. Security

Older technology is usually ridden with security vulnerabilities and other bugs. It's essential that you keep all of your software up-to-date to prevent being hacked.

For example, if your website is running on an outdated version of Wordpress (the most popular Content Management System), there are likely many security vulnerabilities published online that hackers can exploit to their heart's desire.

## 5. Frustrating user experience

Some websites are impossibly hard to use.

+ Broken links
+ Hard to use with touchscreen
+ Slow loading times
+ Popups

## 6. Outdated content

As your business evolves, so should its website's content.

+ Incorrect business hours or location
+ Outdated information
