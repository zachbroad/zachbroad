## Your website is the heart of your online presence
While there are many pieces to the puzzle when it comes to your business's online presence,
your website is the most important.

Not just any website will cut it nowadays. When looking for a new website, keep your eye out for the following.

### Mobile-friendly design
As of 2018, mobile devices make up over [52.2% of all web traffic](https://www.statista.com/statistics/241462/global-mobile-phone-website-traffic-share/).
Just ten years ago, this figure was under 1%. Times have changed -- most of your users will access your site from their phone, not a desktop.

If your website isn't specifically designed for mobile devices, you'll be missing out
on a majority of the market.

### Optimized for Search Engines
Search engine optimization, commonly abbreviated as _SEO_, is the process of ranking your website for specific, targeted keywords.
SEO is the reason why you're able to find what you're looking for on Google.

Without SEO, your website will struggle to be discovered. It's important that a website is optimized in a way that search engines can easily "crawl" the pages to find what people are looking for.

Included with all my websites is a [basic SEO package](/services/seo).

### Fast
Users are more impatient than ever -- did you know that over half of users will leave your page if it takes longer than 3 seconds to load?!

I utilize the following techniques to ensure my websites are some of the fastest around:
- Caching
- Compression of images and other files
- Server-side rendering

My websites utilize a technology known as _server-side rendering_, which drastically cuts down on
the loading times for users. This is especially important when accessing the website from a cell phone with
poor connection.

Don't let a slow website hold your business back.

#### PRPL Compliant
PRPL is a web site architecture developed by Google for building websites and apps that work exceptionally well on smartphones and other devices with unreliable network connections.


PRPL stands for:

* Push critical resources for the initial URL route using <link preload> and http/2.
* Render initial route.
* Pre-cache remaining routes.
* Lazy-load and create remaining routes on demand.

Simply put, websites designed following the PRPL architecture are quickly able to show the users what they came to your site for, and load the unimportant stuff as the user views the page.

What PRPL means for you and your users is that your website will load with minimal delays, especially when the network connection is slow.
With most web traffic nowadays coming from mobile devices, it's important for websites to be designed in a way that allows users with sub-par service to still access your page.
